## What I do (one line)

I find what's actually slowing a founder's team down — and in one 90-minute session they stop scaling around it. They leave knowing what to stop, and the one move to make instead.

## Who it's for

Founders running small teams (roughly 3–20 people) who are moving but not sure they're moving in the right direction. Two flavors:

- **The operator** — has a team, has revenue or users, but the system is cracking. Feels the drag every day. Thinks it's a process problem; it's usually a founder problem.
- **The nervous scaler** — just raised or just started spending. About to hire. Can feel an expensive mistake coming but can't name what it is yet.

## The phrases to listen for

You'll know it's a fit when you hear something like:

- "We're moving but nothing is actually landing."
- "My team is busy but nothing is progressing."
- "I don't know what's actually slowing my team down."
- "We just raised and I'm not sure we're building the right thing."
- "I feel scattered despite progress."
- "I know something's off but I can't put my finger on it."

They don't need to say it exactly. The pattern is: **smart, capable founder who can see something's wrong but can't name it.**

## Who it's NOT for

- Early-stage solo founders with no team yet
- People who find the problem *interesting* but not *expensive* (no urgency, no budget)
- Anyone shopping for coaching, motivation, or a general advisor

## One thing worth knowing about the relationship

Not every conversation ends in a diagnostic sale — and that's fine. When it's not the right fit, Trung says so directly and the relationship holds. People who don't buy now often come back months later, or refer someone who's a better fit. You won't burn a relationship by introducing someone: if it's not the right time, the conversation will be useful anyway and the door stays open.
